TEEN MENTAL HEALTH AND SOCIAL MEDIA USAGE ANALYSIS

Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between social media usage and mental health among teenagers. Using Power BI, the dataset was explored to identify patterns between platform usage, screen time, sleep habits, academic performance, anxiety levels, stress levels, and depression indicators.

The dashboard provides interactive visualizations that help educators, parents, counselors, and researchers understand how social media engagement may affect the well-being of teenagers.

Objectives

- Analyze social media usage patterns among teenagers.
- Examine the impact of social media on mental health indicators such as anxiety, stress, and depression.
- Investigate the relationship between screen time and sleep duration.
- Evaluate the effect of social media usage on academic performance.
- Identify demographic trends across age groups and gender.

Dataset Features

The dataset contains information on:

- Age
- Gender
- Social media platform usage (Instagram, TikTok, Both)
- Daily social media hours
- Sleep hours
- Anxiety level
- Stress level
- Depression indicator
- Addiction level
- Academic performance

Dashboard Insights

1. Platform Usage Distribution

The analysis shows a nearly equal distribution of users across Instagram, TikTok, and teenagers who actively use both platforms.

2. Social Media Usage

Average daily social media usage among teenagers is approximately 4.54 hours, indicating significant exposure to digital platforms.

3. Mental Health Trends

Higher anxiety and stress levels were observed across various age groups and genders, suggesting a potential relationship between social media engagement and mental well-being.

4. Sleep Patterns

The average sleep duration is approximately 6.45 hours, which is below the recommended sleep duration for teenagers, highlighting possible effects of prolonged screen time.

5. Academic Performance

Academic performance remains relatively stable across different addiction levels, with an overall average performance score of approximately 2.99.

6. Gender-Based Analysis

The dashboard compares anxiety and stress levels between male and female teenagers to identify vulnerable groups requiring targeted interventions.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Total Teen Records: 1,200
- Average Social Media Usage: 4.54 Hours
- Average Sleep Duration: 6.45 Hours
- Depression Indicator Sum: 31

Recommendations

Manage Platform Usage

Implement digital wellness programs and encourage healthy screen-time limits, especially for teenagers who spend excessive hours on social media platforms.

Focus on At-Risk Age Groups

Develop age-specific awareness campaigns and educational programs promoting healthy online behavior.

Support Mental Health

Provide counseling services and peer support programs for teenagers experiencing elevated stress and anxiety levels.

Improve Sleep Habits

Promote healthy sleep routines by encouraging reduced device usage before bedtime and educating students on sleep hygiene.

Monitor Academic Impact

Schools should regularly assess how social media usage influences academic performance and provide guidance where necessary.

Conclusion

This analysis highlights the growing influence of social media on teenage mental health and lifestyle habits. By leveraging data-driven insights, stakeholders can implement proactive measures to support healthier digital behavior, improve mental well-being, and foster better academic outcomes among teenagers.
